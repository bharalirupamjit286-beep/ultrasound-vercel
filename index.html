PCPNDT Compliance Notice & Clinic Use Terms

Mandatory legal notice
Disclosure of the sex of the foetus is prohibited under law.
  • This application and its report templates are not to be used for sex determination or sex selection.
  • Use of prenatal diagnostic techniques / ultrasonography must remain only for legally permitted clinical indications and documented reasons.
  • Required statutory declarations, Form F and other applicable records, clinic registration display, consent, and record preservation must be maintained separately by the clinic as required under the PCPNDT Act / Rules and local authority directions.
  • Each obstetric ultrasound report should carry the doctor declaration that the sex of the foetus has neither been detected nor disclosed.
  • This software layer does not by itself guarantee legal compliance; final implementation should be reviewed by the clinic's legal / PCPNDT compliance team.

Technical Disclaimer

NT scan technical limitations
  • This report is based on the sonographic appearances obtained on the date and time of examination and on the image quality achievable during this study.
  • First-trimester NT screening is a risk-assessment examination and does not by itself exclude all chromosomal abnormalities, structural malformations, genetic syndromes, placental disorders, or later-evolving fetal conditions.
  • Detection may be limited by gestational age, fetal position, maternal habitus, abdominal wall scarring, uterine factors, machine resolution, and other technical conditions affecting visualization.
  • A normal NT / marker profile does not guarantee a normal fetus, and abnormal risk markers require correlation with validated screening / diagnostic pathways, clinical findings, and follow-up imaging when indicated.
  • Targeted repeat scan, detailed anomaly scan, fetal echocardiography, genetic counselling, or further diagnostic evaluation may still be necessary depending on clinical context and evolving findings.

Technical Disclaimer

Level II scan technical limitations
  • This report reflects the fetal anatomy and Doppler findings visualized on the present examination only and is dependent on the completeness and quality of views obtained during the scan.
  • Although a routine mid-trimester anomaly scan can detect many major abnormalities, it cannot exclude all congenital, genetic, cardiac, skeletal, placental, or later-evolving fetal abnormalities.
  • Visualization may be limited by fetal lie / movement, maternal habitus, oligohydramnios, uterine / abdominal wall factors, placental position, acoustic shadowing, and other technical constraints; some structures may remain suboptimally assessed.
  • Findings labelled normal are limited to the structures satisfactorily visualized in this examination and should be interpreted along with clinical history, biochemical / genetic screening, and subsequent antenatal follow-up.
  • Where views are incomplete, soft markers are present, or any abnormality is suspected, repeat targeted imaging, fetal echocardiography, fetal medicine review, genetic counselling, or further evaluation may be required.
